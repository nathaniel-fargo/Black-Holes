Types Of Black Holes

Small, medium, and large


Most black holes are just about 10-100 times the mass of our Sun. These black holes become bigger by sucking in surrounding matter, or merging with other black holes.

Normal Black Holes


Supermassive Black Holes

Some black holes grow to extriodinary sizes, but scientists can't find out why, although they do have some theories. These "supermassive" black holes are found at the center of galaxies, often from millions to billion times heavier than our sun.
