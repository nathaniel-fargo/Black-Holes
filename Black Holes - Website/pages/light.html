Light & Black Holes

Where the weird stuff happens

As light comes off of the accretion disk, or the gas ring around the black hole, it bends towards the black hole. Some of this light gets sucked into the black hole, but much of it escapes out to space at a bent angle, creating the distorted image that you see today.

This is the type of image you can get if the accretion disk is around a 5Â° angle, other angles such as the Black Hole shot by NASA, produce traditional looking images of Black Holes.

The best way to create these images are through raycasting, which sends out light rays.
